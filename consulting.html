Common questions

What prospective clients
most often ask.

Is Chiron right for me?

This practice is designed for applicants who are genuinely targeting T14 admissions — not as a long-shot, but as a realistic goal grounded in a competitive profile. If your LSAT score is at or approaching T14 medians, or you are working toward that range, and your GPA is in a defensible position for the schools you're targeting, we likely have a conversation worth having. If your numbers are materially below T14 medians, I will tell you that honestly during the Assessment Session and refer you to resources that are a better fit.

My LSAT score and GPA are strong. Do I still need a consultant?

Strong numbers are necessary but not sufficient at the top schools. Harvard, Yale, Stanford, and Columbia reject large numbers of statistically qualified applicants every cycle. What separates admits from waitlists at that level is almost always the quality of the written application — how clearly and compellingly you have made the case for who you are, why law, and why this school. That is precisely where expert guidance has the most leverage on your outcome.

How is this different from essay editing or a writing coach?

A writing coach can improve your prose. What I bring is something different: I read your application the way an admissions committee reads it — as a lawyer evaluating an argument, as someone who scored 176 and understands the analytical profile being assessed, and as a credentialed educator who knows how to close the gap between where you are and where your writing needs to be. The work starts with strategy — what story to tell, how to position your specific profile for specific schools — and the drafting follows from that.

When should I start?

For applicants targeting fall entry, the spring and summer before you apply are the right time to engage. T14 admissions is rolling — schools that fill early in the cycle close doors that are still open to an October applicant. Starting in July or August gives us time to build a real strategy and execute the written materials without compression. If you are already mid-cycle and need focused help, that conversation is still worth having.

Do you guarantee results?

No — and any consultant who does is telling you what you want to hear. What I guarantee is this: honest assessment of your candidacy before you retain me, expert strategic guidance built on your actual profile rather than generic templates, and personal attention on every piece of work from start to finish. The rest depends on the strength of your application and the decisions of admissions committees I do not control — but my job is to ensure that whatever your profile supports, your application reflects it at the highest possible level.

What does working together actually look like day to day?

Every client has a dedicated workspace where every draft, strategy note, and submission milestone is tracked. Work moves primarily by email and scheduled video sessions — I do not run a texting practice or a calendar-booking portal. You will hear from me within 24 hours on business days, and within four hours during the October–December peak season. Every response comes from me directly.

I need LSAT help as well as admissions consulting. Can you do both?

Yes, and the combination is the strongest version of what this practice offers. A 176 LSAT and eleven years of instruction means I can assess your scoring ceiling honestly, identify exactly what is limiting your score, and work with you to close the gap — then carry that understanding directly into the admissions strategy.
